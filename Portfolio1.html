Top Free Agents Available

This will house the top free agents available on the MLB market.

Jake Arrieta is one of the better pitchers on this winter's free agent market, and his agent, Scott Boras, wants to make that count. Boras has sent teams a 75-page binder on his client, and according to an MLB executive who spoke with ESPN, the expectation is Arrieta is seeking a $200 million deal.

J.D. Martinez sat down with the Boston Red Sox at the Winter Meetings and the Sox still look like the favorites to sign Martinez. The market may be thinning on the outfielder as teams are more reluctant to spend big money with the star-studded class that will be on display in 2018. Originally looking for close to $200 million, Martinez may need to settle for just over $100 million over the course of a five-year deal. The team that signs Martinez will be getting a strong hitter with good plate discipline, who is also a fairly capable outfielder.

The Padres continue to be active this offseason and have “traction” in their pursuit of free-agent first baseman Eric Hosmer, according to Yahoo Sports’ Jeff Passan.

There doesn’t seem to be as much of a market for the 28-year-old Hosmer as was initially projected heading into the offseason, and the Padres have been the club most frequently linked to him. USA TODAY’s Bob Nightengale reported Monday that the Red Sox are now more interested in trading for an impactful hitter than signing Hosmer or J.D. Martinez, and despite the first base opening that could exist in St. Louis, Hosmer hasn’t been connected to the Cardinals very frequently.
